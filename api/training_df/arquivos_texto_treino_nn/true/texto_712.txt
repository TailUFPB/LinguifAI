new york  reuters    a group of  u s  based scientists whose research focuses on arctic wildlife asked two u s  senators in a letter on thursday not to open the national arctic wildlife refuge to oil and gas exploration  according to a copy seen by reuters  the scientists  including several retired former officials from the alaska department of fish   game  the u s  geological survey and the u s  fish   wildlife service  said in a letter to senator lisa murkowski of alaska and senator maria cantwell of washington that drilling for oil and gas in the refuge would be  incompatible with the purposes for which the refuge was established   protecting fish and wildlife populations and the environments in which they live    murkowski  a republican  introduced legislation on wednesday that would create an oil and gas exploration program on the refuge s coastal plain  letting prospectors build any roads and other infrastructure they deemed necessary inside the refuge to carry out their operations  half of the available drilling royalties would go to the state of alaska and half to the federal government  the program s administrator would be required to hold at least two lease sales within  years after the bill became law   the scientists said drilling on the coastal plain would be particularly harmful because it contains a  unique compression  of habitats supporting animals like polar bears  grizzly bears  wolverines  representing  the greatest wildlife diversity of any protected area above the arctic circle   in a reuters interview  cantwell praised the scientists for
